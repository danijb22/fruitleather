Starting this post off with the admission that our drummer and friend
Shravan Raghuram is a freak of nature and reminds me of that Octupus
guy from House on the Rock.

You know the one.

As an incredibly talented drummer and incredibly kind person, Shravan's
presence is demanded all around the city. He works his ass off and
spends his gas money to do the sort of thing he loves to do and does it
quite well. (See this , and this , and this ).

His longest-term project, "The Data Waves" is a classic mid-western
jazz-jam funk-fest filled with a roster of incredibly close and talented
players, which is readily apperntly in their work. I use a "happy light"
when winter folds itself over on us and steals the grace of sunlight.
Regulating my own exposure to sunlight is a ritual I've worked hard at
for a while, and one of the biggest tools in that belt is the light,
swaying motion of summer music. I'm here to celebrate the Data Wave's
latest release 'Puesta Del Sol' for being just that. It's a really
sweet record appreciating the love you have with your love, and
catching the brightness of life when you're present in it. The EP is
easy, bass-heavy (thanks Aditya), and coherent, refusing to overdo
a single thing. Big ups Shravan this is gorgeous !

- Dani
